Mona Lisa by Leonardo da Vinci

The Mona Lisa is a famous painting by Leonardo da Vinci, depicting a woman with a mysterious smile. It is considered an archetypal masterpiece of the Italian Renaissance and one of the most valuable paintings in the world.

The painting is believed to portray Lisa del Giocondo, the wife of a wealthy Florentine merchant, Francesco del Giocondo. Leonardo was commissioned to paint her portrait in 1503, but he never delivered it to the Giocondo family. He kept working on it until his death in 1519, and left it to his favorite apprentice, Salaì.
